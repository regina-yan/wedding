Frequently Asked Questions

Staying at Camp Glen Brook

How much does lodging cost at the camp?

Staying at the camp is free.

When is check in/out?

You may arrive anytime after 12pm on Friday, and we ask that you clean up your space and head out around 12pm on Sunday.

What are the accommodations like at the camp?

There is indoor lodging in the main building, A-frames (1st image), and outdoor cabins (2nd image - we took this photo in the winter, so disregard the snow). The beds are twin size, most of them bunked. There are shared bathrooms, showers, and drinkable water in the main building and A-frames; and an outdoor outhouse, showers, and drinkable water for the outdoor cabins. The outdoor cabins have a mini rec hall cabin too. Rooms will have 2-10 beds each. We'll do our best to put people in rooms with friends / compatible folks, but please reach out if you have any concerns or requests!

Do the cabins have heating or A/C?

The Main building and wing have heating and A/C, but the A-frames and outdoor cabins do not. The A-frames are insulated, so warmth should not be an issue at this time of year.

What do I need to bring to stay at the camp?

The beds don't have any linens, so you'll need to bring your own. This is what we recommend for a packing list (in addition to clothing, toiletries, etc.):

Necessary

  • sleeping bag, or a fitted sheet + comforter
  • pillow
  • towel

Recommended

  • swimsuit

I'm not very outdoorsy but I do want to stay at camp!

We will try very hard to match people with accomodations we think will suit them best - if you have any concerns, please reach out to us! Please note that the main building rooms will probably be for family/relatives. Shep would like to note that the outdoor cabins are incredibly nice and would be his preferred choice.

Allergies?

There are farm animals on the site (chickens, pigs, sheep, goats). We'll have Benadryl / Claritin handy, but let us know if you have other concerns.
